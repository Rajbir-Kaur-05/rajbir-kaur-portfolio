Module 1

01

Terminology Matters

Learning

From this topic, I learned that the words we choose are very important because they show respect for people's identities, cultures, and histories. I now understand that using the correct terminology when referring to Indigenous Peoples is not only about being accurate but also about creating an inclusive and respectful learning environment. I also learned that some terms have a painful colonial history and should only be used in specific legal or historical contexts.

As an educator, this learning reminded me that my language influences how students think and speak about others. I want to model respectful language in my classroom, gently correct misunderstandings, and help students understand why the words they use matter.

Why I Selected This

This learning was meaningful to me because I learned that the words we choose are very important. They show respect for people's identities, cultures, and histories. It helped me understand that using respectful and accurate language is an important part of creating an inclusive classroom.

How I Will Apply This in My Classroom

  • Use respectful and accurate terminology when referring to Indigenous Peoples.
  • Model respectful language in everyday classroom conversations.
  • Explain why using correct terminology is important.
  • Gently correct students when incorrect or outdated terms are used.
  • Choose classroom books and resources that use respectful and accurate language.
  • Create a classroom environment where every student feels respected, valued, and included.
02

First Peoples Principles of Learning

Learning

The First Peoples Principles of Learning helped me understand that learning is more than gaining knowledge. It is built through relationships, respect, reflection, and connection to the land, community, and culture. I learned that every learner is unique and that learning happens in different ways and at different times.

Why I Selected This

I chose the First Peoples Principles of Learning because they helped me understand that learning is about much more than academic success. These principles emphasize the importance of relationships, respect, identity, community, and connection to the land. They align with the kind of classroom I want to create, where every student feels valued, respected, connected, and supported.

How I Will Apply This in My Classroom

  • Build positive relationships with local Indigenous communities, Elders, and Knowledge Keepers.
  • Integrate Indigenous perspectives across all subject areas, not just during special events.
  • Create opportunities for land-based and place-based learning.
  • Help students develop respect for the land, Indigenous cultures, and their local community.
  • Recognize that every student learns in their own unique way and at their own pace.
  • Foster a caring classroom where students learn kindness, respect, and responsibility.
03

The Role of Witnessing

Learning

From this topic, I learned that witnessing is much more than simply watching or listening. It is an important responsibility that involves listening with care, remembering what was shared, and respectfully passing that knowledge on to others. I also learned that witnessing is based on respect, accountability, and preserving knowledge for future generations.

Why I Selected This

I chose this as one of my key learnings because it changed my understanding of what it means to truly listen. I learned that witnessing is an active responsibility that involves paying attention, reflecting on what we hear, and sharing knowledge respectfully.

How I Will Apply This in My Classroom

  • Teach students the importance of listening with respect and attention.
  • Use witnessing activities during class discussions, storytelling, and group work.
  • Invite students to reflect on and summarize key ideas after lessons.
  • Create opportunities for students to listen to different perspectives and experiences.
  • Model respectful listening and thoughtful communication in everyday classroom interactions.
04

Overall Reflection

Module 1

This module has helped me develop a much deeper understanding of Indigenous histories, cultures, and ways of knowing. At the beginning, I had limited knowledge about many of these topics. Through each topic, I have gained new perspectives that will shape both my personal growth and my future teaching practice.

One of my biggest learnings was understanding the true purpose of land acknowledgements. I now recognize that they are not simply words spoken at the beginning of an event. They are a meaningful way to honor the First Peoples, recognize their enduring relationship with the land, and remind us of our shared responsibility to care for it and continue the work of reconciliation.

I also learned that terminology matters. The words we choose reflect our respect for people's identities, cultures, and histories. As an educator, I understand that using respectful and accurate language helps create an inclusive classroom where every student feels valued and respected.

The First Peoples Principles of Learning changed the way I think about education. They taught me that learning is built through relationships, respect, reflection, storytelling, community, and connection to the land. I now see that learning is not only about academic achievement but also about supporting the well-being of the whole person.

Learning about the role of witnessing showed me that listening is an active responsibility. Witnessing involves paying careful attention, reflecting on what we learn, and respectfully sharing that knowledge with others. This has encouraged me to create classroom opportunities where students listen thoughtfully, reflect on their learning, and value different perspectives.

The Indigenous Ally Toolkit reminded me that being an ally is more than saying the right words - it requires meaningful action. Being an ally means continuing to learn, listening to Indigenous voices, speaking up against discrimination, challenging stereotypes, and helping create an inclusive and respectful environment for everyone.

As I move forward in my teaching career, I am committed to continuing my learning journey. I will respectfully integrate Indigenous perspectives throughout the curriculum, build positive relationships with Indigenous communities whenever possible, and create learning experiences that help students develop respect for the land, Indigenous Peoples, and one another. This course has given me valuable knowledge and inspired me to become a more thoughtful, inclusive, and culturally responsive educator.
